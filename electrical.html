What Do Electrical Engineers Do?

Electronics engineers design, develop, and test electronic systems and components that power a wide range of modern technologies. They work with circuits, semiconductors, sensors, and embedded systems to create everything from consumer electronics and communication devices to industrial automation systems and aerospace technologies. Their work forms the backbone of innovations in computing, telecommunications, robotics, and beyond. Using principles from physics, mathematics, and computer science, electronics engineers build devices such as smartphones, microprocessors, wireless transmitters, and control systems. They also develop the hardware behind smart technologies, including Internet of Things (IoT) devices, medical equipment, and renewable energy systems like solar inverters and smart grids. In addition to hardware design, they often collaborate with software engineers to integrate firmware and ensure seamless operation of complex electronic systems. Their responsibilities may include prototyping, testing, system optimization, and ensuring compliance with safety and performance standards. Electronics engineers are employed across industries, including consumer tech, defense, automotive, aerospace, healthcare, and telecommunications. Whether improving signal processing, increasing energy efficiency, or advancing wireless communication, electronics engineers play a central role in driving technological progress and shaping the way we live and interact with the world.

Job Titles in Electrical Engineering

Job Title Role Description Typical Salary
Power Systems Engineer Designs and manages the distribution of electrical power in power grids and renewable systems. $75,000 – $110,000 per year
Electronics Design Engineer Develops and tests circuit boards and embedded systems for various electronic products. $70,000 – $105,000 per year
Control Systems Engineer Designs automation systems for industrial and manufacturing processes. $80,000 – $115,000 per year
Communications Engineer Works on wireless communication systems, network protocols, and telecom infrastructure. $78,000 – $120,000 per year
Hardware Engineer Designs and develops computer hardware components like processors and memory devices. $85,000 – $130,000 per year
Renewable Energy Engineer Focuses on integrating solar, wind, and other renewable sources into power systems. $75,000 – $115,000 per year

Salaries vary based on location, experience, education, and sector (private industry, research, or government).

Industries Where Electrical Engineers Work

  • Power and Energy: Utilities, grid infrastructure, renewable energy systems, smart grids.
  • Consumer Electronics: Designing smartphones, audio devices, wearables, and home automation products.
  • Telecommunications: Developing and maintaining wireless networks, satellites, and broadband systems.
  • Manufacturing and Robotics: Automation systems, robotics control, and industrial machinery.
  • Transportation: Electrical systems in electric vehicles (EVs), rail systems, avionics, and marine tech.
  • Healthcare Technology: Creating devices like pacemakers, MRI machines, and patient monitoring tools.
  • Research and Development: Academic and industrial R&D labs for innovation and new tech development.

Skills You'll Need

Electrical engineers require a solid foundation in theory, as well as practical problem-solving and collaboration skills:

  • Circuit Design and Analysis: Understanding how circuits behave and using tools like SPICE for simulation.
  • Embedded Systems and Microcontrollers: Programming and integrating microchips in various devices.
  • Signal Processing: Analyzing, filtering, and interpreting electronic signals in audio, video, or data systems.
  • Power Systems and Electronics: Designing high- and low-voltage systems for power management and safety.
  • Mathematical and Analytical Thinking: Using calculus, differential equations, and linear algebra daily.
  • Communication and Teamwork: Explaining ideas clearly and collaborating across disciplines.

Recommended Online Courses

  • Introduction to Electronics – georgia institute of technology Coursera

    This course introduces students to the basic components of electronics: diodes, transistors, and op amps. It covers the basic operation and some common applications.

  • Electric Power Systems – University at Buffalo on Coursera

    This course familiarizes you with standards and policies of the electric utility industry, and provides you with basic vocabulary used in the business. It introduces the electric power system, from generation of the electricity all the way to the wall plug. You will learn about the segments of the system, and common components like power cables and transformers.

  • The Complete Electronics Course: Analog Hardware Designy – Udemy

    Starting with Ohm's Law, we will progressively build more and more complex circuits.

Beginner Projects You Can Try

Build a Flashing LED Circuit

Use a 555 timer chip to make LEDs blink in patterns. Learn about resistors, capacitors, and timing control.

Create a Mini Solar Charger

Build a simple solar-powered USB charger and explore how solar panels convert light into usable energy.

Arduino Home Automation

Use an Arduino board to control lights, sensors, or fans through your smartphone. A great intro to embedded systems.
